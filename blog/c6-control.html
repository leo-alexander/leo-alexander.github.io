Chapter 5 of "Search Inside Yourself" deals with triggers - when a seemingly small situation causes a disproportionally large emotional response in us. It could be identified by body reactions (shallow breathing, rapid heartbeat, etc). They almost always have long histories behind the, and often bring back something from the past.

The Siberian North Railroad is a practise for dealing with triggers. It has five steps: Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, and Respond (SBNRR = SiBerian North Rail-Road).

  • Stop: Simple and important.
  • Breathe: Reinforces the pause.
  • Notice: Bring attention to the body - notice changes in temperature and tension.
  • Reflect: Understand the emotion and its origins.
  • Respond: Think of the most positive way to respond.
