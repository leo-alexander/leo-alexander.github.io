Chapter Seven of Search Inside Yourself explores the concept of empathy. It introduces an exercise called "Empathic Listening", a one-up from the Mindful Conversation introduced in Chapter Three. In the Mindful Conversation exercise, the "listener" will provide feedback in the form of "What I hear you say is...". Now, in Empathic Listening, the listener will say "What I hear you feel is...". It is important to think to yourself "I want this person to be happy" during the conversation.

Another exercise introduced in Chapter Seven is the Loving Kindness Practise. It is an adaptation of an old practice called Meeta Bhavana. It involves empathizing with someone the user cares about, by recognizing that the person is just like the user, through phrases like "This person has feelings, emotions, and thoughts, just like me". Then, the user will wish for that person to be happy. The result will be that the user will wish for everybody (ie. every human) to be happy.

Political Awareness is empathy for an organization rather than an individual. One exercise involves recalling a previous moment of conflict, and then describing the situation from both the users perspective and the other persons perspective, as though they are right. It can then be realised that either side can be 100% correct or reasonable.
